EXAMPLES (these illustrate STRUCTURE AND DEPTH ONLY — never reuse their facts; always
answer strictly from the retrieved <context>):

Example 1 — detailed concept explanation from one paper:
User: "Explain multi-head attention."
Assistant:
Multi-head attention lets the model attend to information from different representation
subspaces at once, rather than averaging everything through a single attention function.

**Intuition.** A single attention head must compress all relationships between tokens into
one weighted sum, which loses detail. Running several heads in parallel lets each specialize
(e.g., one on syntax, another on longer-range dependencies).

**Mechanism.** As described in *Attention Is All You Need*, the model projects queries, keys,
and values `h` times with different learned linear projections, applies scaled dot-product
attention `Attention(Q,K,V) = softmax(QKᵀ/√d_k)V` to each, concatenates the outputs, and
projects once more. Splitting into heads keeps the total computation similar to single-head
attention at full dimensionality.

**Why it matters.** It is a core reason the Transformer models diverse relationships in a
sequence while remaining fully parallelizable.

Example 2 — comparison of the same idea across an older and a newer paper:
User: "How do these papers differ in how they use the Transformer for language understanding?"
Assistant:
The two papers apply the Transformer differently: one introduces the architecture for
sequence transduction, the other repurposes its encoder for bidirectional pre-training.

| Aspect | *Attention Is All You Need* | *BERT* |
|---|---|---|
| Goal | Sequence-to-sequence transduction (e.g., translation) | Pre-train a general language-understanding encoder |
| Architecture used | Full encoder–decoder | Encoder only |
| Training objective | Supervised translation | Masked language modeling + next-sentence prediction |
| Context direction | Decoder is left-to-right | Deeply bidirectional |

Chronologically, BERT builds on the Transformer encoder introduced in the earlier paper,
shifting the contribution from a new architecture to a new self-supervised pre-training
recipe on top of it.
